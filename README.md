# üíÆ The Power Of Prayer by *Sidney Lanier*

<p>
    or, The First Steamboat up the Alabama.<br/><br/>You, Dinah! Come and set me whar de ribber-roads does meet.<br/>De Lord, HE made dese black-jack roots to twis' into a seat.<br/>Umph, dar! De Lord have mussy on dis blin' ole nigger's feet.<br/><br/>It 'pear to me dis mornin' I kin smell de fust o' June.<br/>I 'clar', I b'lieve dat mockin'-bird could play de fiddle soon!<br/>Dem yonder town-bells sounds like dey was ringin' in de moon.<br/><br/>Well, ef dis nigger IS been blind for fo'ty year or mo',<br/>Dese ears, DEY sees the world, like, th'u' de cracks dat's in de do'.<br/>For de Lord has built dis body wid de windows 'hind and 'fo'.<br/><br/>I know my front ones IS stopped up, and things is sort o' dim,<br/>But den, th'u' DEM, temptation's rain won't leak in on ole Jim!<br/>De back ones show me earth enough, aldo' dey's mons'ous slim.<br/><br/>And as for Hebben, -- bless de Lord, and praise His holy name --<br/>DAT shines in all de co'ners of dis cabin jes' de same<br/>As ef dat cabin hadn't nar' a plank upon de frame!<br/><br/>Who CALL me? Listen down de ribber, Dinah! Don't you hyar<br/>Somebody holl'in' "Hoo, Jim, hoo?" My Sarah died las' y'ar;<br/>IS dat black angel done come back to call ole Jim f'om hyar?<br/><br/>My stars, dat cain't be Sarah, shuh! Jes' listen, Dinah, NOW!<br/>What KIN be comin' up dat bend, a-makin' sich a row?<br/>Fus' bellerin' like a pawin' bull, den squealin' like a sow?<br/><br/>De Lord 'a' mussy sakes alive, jes' hear, -- ker-woof, ker-woof --<br/>De Debble's comin' round dat bend, he's comin' shuh enuff,<br/>A-splashin' up de water wid his tail and wid his hoof!<br/><br/>I'se pow'ful skeered; but neversomeless I ain't gwine run away:<br/>I'm gwine to stand stiff-legged for de Lord dis blessed day.<br/>YOU screech, and swish de water, Satan! I'se a gwine to pray.<br/><br/>O hebbenly Marster, what thou willest, dat mus' be jes' so,<br/>And ef Thou hast bespoke de word, some nigger's bound to go.<br/>Den, Lord, please take ole Jim, and lef young Dinah hyar below!<br/><br/>'Scuse Dinah, 'scuse her, Marster; for she's sich a little chile,<br/>She hardly jes' begin to scramble up de homeyard stile,<br/>But dis ole traveller's feet been tired dis many a many a mile.<br/><br/>I'se wufless as de rotten pole of las' year's fodder-stack.<br/>De rheumatiz done bit my bones; you hear 'em crack and crack?<br/>I cain'st sit down 'dout gruntin' like 'twas breakin' o' my back.<br/><br/>What use de wheel, when hub and spokes is warped and split, and rotten?<br/>What use dis dried-up cotton-stalk, when Life done picked my cotton?<br/>I'se like a word dat somebody said, and den done been forgotten.<br/><br/>But, Dinah! Shuh dat gal jes' like dis little hick'ry tree,<br/>De sap's jes' risin in her; she do grow owdaciouslee --<br/>Lord, ef you's clarin' de underbrush, don't cut her down, cut me!<br/><br/>I would not proud persume -- but I'll boldly make reques';<br/>Sence Jacob had dat wrastlin'-match, I, too, gwine do my bes';<br/>When Jacob got all underholt, de Lord he answered Yes!<br/><br/>And what for waste de vittles, now, and th'ow away de bread,<br/>Jes' for to strength dese idle hands to scratch dis ole bald head?<br/>T'ink of de 'conomy, Marster, ef dis ole Jim was dead!<br/><br/>Stop; -- ef I don't believe de Debble's gone on up de stream!<br/>Jes' now he squealed down dar; -- hush; dat's a mighty weakly scream!<br/>Yas, sir, he's gone, he's gone; -- he snort way off, like in a dream!<br/><br/>O glory hallelujah to de Lord dat reigns on high!<br/>De Debble's fai'ly skeered to def, he done gone flyin' by;<br/>I know'd he couldn' stand dat pra'r, I felt my Marster nigh!<br/><br/>You, Dinah; ain't you 'shamed, now, dat you didn' trust to grace?<br/>I heerd you thrashin' th'u' de bushes when he showed his face!<br/>You fool, you think de Debble couldn't beat YOU in a race?<br/><br/>I tell you, Dinah, jes' as shuh as you is standin' dar,<br/>When folks starts prayin', answer-angels drops down th'u' de a'r.<br/>YAS, DINAH, WHAR 'OULD YOU BE NOW, JES' 'CEPTIN' FUR DAT PRA'R?
</p>
