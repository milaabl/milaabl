# üíÆ I'd Mourn the Hopes by *Thomas Moore*

<p>
    I'd mourn the hopes that leave me,<br/>If thy smiles had left me too;<br/>I'd weep when friends deceive me,<br/>If thou wert, like them, untrue.<br/>But while I've thee before me,<br/>With heart so warm and eyes so bright,<br/>No clouds can linger o'er me,<br/>That smile turns them all to light.<br/><br/>'Tis not in fate to harm me,<br/>While fate leaves thy love to me:<br/>'Tis not in joy to charm me,<br/>Unless joy be shared with thee.<br/>One minute's dream about thee<br/>Were worth a long, an endless year<br/>Of waking bliss without thee,<br/>My own love, my only dear!<br/><br/>And though the hope be gone, love,<br/>That long sparkled o'er our way,<br/>Oh! we shall journey on, love,<br/>More safely, without its ray.<br/>Far better lights shall win me,<br/>Along the path I've yet to roam --<br/>The mind that burns within me,<br/>And pure smiles from thee at home.<br/><br/>Thus, when the lamp that lighted<br/>The traveller at first goes out,<br/>He feels awhile benighted,<br/>And looks round in fear and doubt.<br/>But soon, the prospect clearing,<br/>By cloudless starlight on he treads,<br/>And thinks no lamp so cheering<br/>As the light which Heaven sheds.
</p>
